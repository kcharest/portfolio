cut & paste apparel website

the project

Cut & Paste apparel is a brand idea I want to conceptualize that is entirely web based. This one page website was created with the purpose of both providing users with an overview of the Cut & Paste brand as well as providing a product showcase that provides users a way to buy directly from the brand.

the problem

The design problems presented were fairly straightforward. How could I make a responsive one page website that both presented this brand to users in an interesting and engaging way but also create a very simple and intuitive experience.


To better understand the market I began by conducting a competitive analysis and evaluating the competitors in this online retail market. The important questions were to find out the strengths of their websites and how they appealed to users as well as questioning their faults and what missed opportunities there may have been.

*sample SWOT analysis based on similar websites

the process

The design process for this project began with brand exploration. It was first important to establish the apparel brand and its aesthetic if I was going to be able to build a proper showcase website for it. I mind mapped and created an moodboard to base my design aesthetic off of.


Once the brand and products were created I began to plan out my wireframe. Since Cut & Paste is a one page website it was important to structure the website to work in sections as well as a whole. As with all my projects I begin with basic handrawn sketches.

basic sketches
wireframes

Hi-Fi wireframes were then created in Sketch to be tested with potential users for feedback.

Once the wireframe was completed and tested with a potential user base it was time to create the responsive website.

It was important to not only keep site simple and focus on showcasing the apparel, but also to engage the user. Adding simple jquery elements to the website such as the smooth scroll and the imageswitcher are small touches that provided interactivity.

high fidelity mockups

the conclusion

The Cut & Paste website was a fun experience to create and pushed my front end design skills which is always rewarding in the end. The website is simple and yet perfectly showcases the brand items as was intended.
